Course Outline – STATS 768 : Longitudinal Data Analysis
2024 Semester Two (15 points)

Grade Achieved

A+ (First in Course Award)

Course Prescription

Exploration and regression modelling of longitudinal and clustered data, especially in the health sciences: mixed models, marginal models, dropout, causal inference.

Course Overview

This course introduces longitudinal data analysis with a focus on repeated measures and clustered data, particularly in the health sciences. Topics include data exploration, summary statistics over time, and advanced regression techniques such as GLMMs, GEEs, marginal structural models, and survival analysis. Students will learn R and SAS and interpret output for real-world studies. It is especially relevant for biostatistics, clinical trials, global health, and census analysis.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

  1. Distinguish between dependent and independent data (Capabilities 3, 4).
  2. Apply mixed and GLMM techniques to real problems (Capabilities 3, 4, 5).
  3. Apply model building and selection techniques (Capabilities 3, 4, 5).
  4. Implement analysis plans for clustered/longitudinal data (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6).
  5. Present results clearly to non-statistical audiences (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6, 7).
  6. Relate statistical findings back to research objectives (Capabilities 5, 6, 7).
  7. Demonstrate practical proficiency in R and SAS (Capabilities 3, 4, 5).
