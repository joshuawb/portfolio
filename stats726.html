Course Prescription

Stationary processes, modelling and estimation in the time domain, forecasting and spectral analysis.

Course Overview

STATS 726 provides a general introduction to the theory of time series and prediction, covering stationary processes, moving average and autoregressive models (ARMA), modelling and estimation in the time domain, seasonal and non‑stationary models, forecasting, and spectral analysis. It is particularly suitable for students in statistics, economics and finance, engineering, and the physical sciences, and supplies a solid theoretical foundation for postgraduate study.  Skills gained are especially relevant for data scientists working on time‑series forecasting.

Capabilities Developed

  • Capability 3 – Knowledge and Practice
  • Capability 5 – Solution Seeking
  • Capability 6 – Communication

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

  1. Identify the main components and features present in a time series (Capability 3).
  2. Describe the theory behind stationary processes (Capability 3).
  3. Apply (seasonal) ARIMA models to univariate time‑series analysis (Capability 5).
  4. Perform time‑series forecasting (Capability 5).
  5. Use R for the analysis and prediction of time series (Capability 3).
  6. Perform inference in the time/frequency domain (Capability 5).
  7. Communicate solutions to time‑series problems in context (Capability 6).
