Course Outline – STATS 779 : Professional Skills for Statisticians
2024 Semester One (15 points)

Grade Achieved

A+ (First in Course Award)

Course Prescription

Statistical software, data management, data integrity, data transfer, file processing, symbolic manipulation, document design and presentation, oral presentation, professional ethics.

Course Overview

This course introduces software tools and professional practices essential to statistical research and applied work. Emphasis is on command-line interfaces and reproducibility, focusing on data processing, reporting, ethics, and communication. It is suited to students intending to produce dissertations or engage in professional statistical work. Prior experience with R is expected or must be gained through self-study.

Capabilities Developed

  • Capability 3 – Knowledge and Practice
  • Capability 4 – Critical Thinking
  • Capability 5 – Solution Seeking
  • Capability 6 – Communication
  • Capability 8 – Ethics and Professionalism

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

  1. Create human-readable and perceptually effective data visualisations (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6).
  2. Communicate statistical concepts clearly in writing and orally (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6).
  3. Write reproducible code to support data analysis (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6, 8).
  4. Summarise and process unstructured data using regular expressions (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6).
  5. Identify and reflect on examples of unethical conduct (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6, 8).
  6. Demonstrate understanding of relational databases and their use (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6).
