Course Prescription

Fundamentals of exploratory analysis of multivariate data, with emphasis on the use of statistical software and reporting of results. Topics include techniques for data display, dimension reduction and ordination, cluster analysis, multivariate ANOVA and associated methods.

Course Overview

This course covers analysis of datasets with multiple response variables, including high‑dimensional cases with hundreds or thousands of variables. Students focus on software implementation, interpretation and communication of results, culminating in a project where they analyse a dataset of their choice and deliver written and oral reports. Techniques taught are especially relevant for Biology, Ecology, Chemistry, Finance, Marketing and other applied fields.

  • Capability 5 – Solution Seeking
  • Capability 6 – Communication

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

  1. Select an appropriate multivariate procedure for a given dataset and research question, with justification (Capabilities 3, 4, 5).
  2. Correctly perform multivariate procedures in statistical software and explain analytical choices (Capabilities 3, 4, 6).
  3. Identify and check assumptions underlying multivariate analyses (Capabilities 3, 4, 6).
  4. Create informative visualisations of multivariate data with appropriate labels and captions (Capabilities 3, 6).
  5. Summarise conclusions from multivariate analyses in non‑technical language, orally and in writing (Capabilities 3, 6).
