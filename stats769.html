Course Prescription

Databases, SQL, scripting, distributed computation, other data technologies.

Course Overview

STATS 769 is intended to provide students with computing concepts and skills involved in the acquisition, manipulation, and analysis of large and/or complex data sets. A secondary aim is to give students practice in applying data mining and machine learning techniques, data mining tools, working with large datasets, computer clusters, parallel and large memory computing. The course will assume a certain amount of basic computing knowledge and familiarity with R, such as might be obtained by completion of STATS 220, STATS 380, or STATS 782. This course is relevant for a career in Data Science.

Course Requirements

Prerequisite: 15 points from STATS 220, 369, 380 and 15 points from ENGSCI 314, STATS 201, 207, 208, 707.

  • Capability 5 – Solution Seeking
  • Capability 6 – Communication
  • Capability 7 – Collaboration

Graduate Profile: Master of Science

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

  1. Apply the computing concepts and skills required to work with large and/or complicated data sets (Capabilities 3, 4, 5).
  2. Perform data mining via an introduction to applicable techniques (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6).
  3. Work with data formats and distributed systems (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6).
  4. Describe the principles underlying parallel and large memory computing (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6).
  5. Apply written communication skills to communicate knowledge clearly and succinctly (Capabilities 3, 4, 5, 6, 7).

Workload Expectations

This is a standard 15‑point course. Students are expected to spend ~150 hours over the semester.

  • 2 hours of lectures
  • A 2‑hour tutorial
  • 2 hours reviewing course content
  • ~4 hours on assignments and/or test preparation

Delivery Mode

  • Campus experience; attendance expected at scheduled activities including lectures and labs.
  • Lectures will be available as recordings; other activities (including labs) will not be recorded.
  • No live online events (including group discussions/tutorials).
  • Test and exam are on campus; attendance required.
  • Activities follow a standard weekly timetable.
